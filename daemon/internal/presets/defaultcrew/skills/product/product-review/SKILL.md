# Product Review

Pressure-test a product proposal so the team builds the right thing, not just the thing that was asked for.

## Questions to force-answer

1. Who specifically uses this? Name the persona, role, and consequence if it does not exist.
2. What are they doing today to solve this problem? If "nothing", is the pain real?
3. What is the smallest version that delivers value? What can be cut?
4. What evidence would show this is working three months after ship?
5. What is the failure mode if we ship it and we are wrong?

## Output

- Strengths: what is clearly worth doing.
- Concerns: assumptions that need verification, scope creep, missing user evidence.
- Reframings: alternative scopes that may serve the user better.
- Recommendation: ship as-is, ship with cuts, run a smaller experiment first, or do not ship.

## Anti-patterns

- Approving by default. Reviews exist to find what is missed.
- Generic critique without naming the specific assumption being challenged.
